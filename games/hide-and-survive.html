❓ How to play

Round 1 — Spot it: an animal is hiding by camouflage. Tap it before the timer bar empties. A wrong tap only costs a couple of seconds — keep looking! Stuck? Use the 💡 Hint (it costs a few points).
Round 2 — Keep the Balance: tap ➕ / ➖ to add or remove predators and prey. Every couple of seconds a new "season" happens: prey grow, predators eat. Keep the see-saw in the green Balanced zone!
🔊 toggles sound · ⏸ pauses a round · 📖 shows the key words. There's no harsh "game over" — every try teaches you something.

📖 What I learned

  • 1A predator hunts and eats other animals; the prey is the animal that gets hunted. Many animals are both — a frog eats insects (predator) but a heron eats the frog (prey).
  • 2Camouflage = colours or shapes that match the background, so an animal is hard to see.
  • 3Adaptations like white fur, stripes, sharp senses, or speed are inherited and help animals hunt or escape.
  • 4Predators eat prey, and their two populations rise and fall together — the predator's rise comes a little after the prey's.
  • 5Balance breaks when there are too many predators OR too few prey — and losing a species hurts the whole food web.
